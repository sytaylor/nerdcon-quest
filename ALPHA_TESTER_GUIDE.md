# NerdCon Quest Alpha Tester Guide

Thanks for testing NerdCon Quest. This is an early alpha of a mobile-first event companion for Fintech NerdCon.

Please test on your phone if possible. iPhone Safari or Android Chrome are the most useful.

## Goal

We want to know if the app helps you do three things:

- Plan what to attend.
- Meet useful people.
- Understand the game layer without explanation.

This is not a design approval test. If something is confusing, slow, broken, or pointless, that is useful feedback.

## Before You Start

Use a real email address you can access on your phone.

Set aside 15-20 minutes.

If the app asks for camera permission, allow it if you are testing QR scanning. If you do not want to allow camera access, tell us what happened and continue with the rest of the test.

## Test Script

1. Open the app link on your phone.
2. Sign in with the magic link.
3. Complete your profile.
4. Choose a quest line: Builder, Operator, or Explorer.
5. Read the Start Here card.
6. Tell us what you think your Nerd Number is for.
7. Open Quests and add at least three sessions to your schedule.
8. Open Community and browse people.
9. Try one social action:
   - Send a connection request.
   - Accept a request if one is waiting.
   - Join or inspect a squad.
   - Start or accept a DM if available.
10. Open Profile and find your QR code.
11. Try to scan another tester's QR code if someone is nearby.
12. Check whether your XP, missions, or leaderboard changed.

## What To Report

Please send feedback in this format:

```text
Device:
Browser:
Approx time spent:
Did you understand Nerd Number? yes/no
Did you know what to do first? yes/no
Most confusing moment:
Most useful feature:
Bug or broken screen:
Would you use this at an event? why/why not:
```

## Things We Care About Most

- Did the first screen tell you what to do?
- Did the app feel useful or just game-like?
- Did you trust the social features?
- Did any button lead nowhere?
- Did anything feel creepy, spammy, or too public?
- Did the app break after refresh, sign-out, or bad network?

## Known Alpha Edges

- Some event data may still be placeholder.
- The venue map may be a preview rather than the final floor plan.
- The goal is to test the product loop, not final event operations.

If you get stuck, write down the exact screen and what you expected to happen next.
